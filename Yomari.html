Yomari



Yomari is a Newari dish that is consumed in Nepal during the Yomari Punhi festival. It represents the full moon day, or Purnima, of Poush, the month of the Nepalese Era Calendar. Ya-Mari is another name for it. 'Ya' signifies similar, while 'Mari' denotes delicate. Additionally, it is made for important events like baby showers and birthdays. It is also a very well-liked dish in Nepal.
Yomari are steamed dumplings filled with "Chhaku," a sweet substance consisting of palm sugar, molasses, or jaggery. Yomari leftovers make a fantastic breakfast substitute as well.

INGREDIENTS


1.2 cups of rice flour
2.1/3 cup of all-purpose flour
3.2 cups jaggery
4.1 tbsp sesame seed
5.2 tbsps ghee (clarified butter)/oil
6.1/2 cup milk
7.1/2 cup warm water

Directions

steps:

Preparing dough

Mix rice flour and all-purpose flour in a bowl, gradually add warm water, knead to soften, cover, and let rest for 10 minutes.

Preparing Jaggery

Heat a saucepan, add jaggery and sesame seed, and stir. Melt jaggery, add milk, and pinch between fingers. Pour into a bowl and add fried sesame seeds. Stir until ready.

Making Yomari

To make Yomari, moisten a hand with oil and shape a small dough ball into a cone-shaped shape. Dip your forefinger in warm water to create a cavity, then add melted jaggery and sesame seeds. Close the upper part of the cone using your forefinger and thumb. Steam for 10-15 minutes

Yomari can now be served with some aachar since it is now ready to be serve.
